The Indian Tiger

The most dangerous king of the indian forest

Characteristics:

The Bengal tiger's coat is yellow to light orange, with stripes ranging from dark brown to black; the belly and the interior parts of the limbs are white, and the tail is orange with black rings. The greatest skull length of a tiger is 351 mm (13.8 in) in males and 293 mm (11.5 in) in females.[19] It has exceptionally stout teeth. Its canines are 7.5 to 10 cm (3.0 to 3.9 in) long and thus the longest among all cats.


Threats:

  • Poaching
  • Human Tiger Conflict

Scientific Classification:

Kingdom: Animalia
Class: Mammalia
Order: Carnivora
